## Decision Three

### Financial Structure

> [!insight] The Core Question
> The same budget buys two very different days depending on where it lands. The question isn't "how much" yet — it's "what should the money be doing?"

#### A local wedding
- More guests, lower travel barrier — turnout climbs.
- Budget concentrates on decor, food, and hospitality.
- Per-guest spend is lower; total can still grow with the headcount.

#### A destination wedding
- Fewer guests, but a richer, multi-day experience for those who come.
- Budget shifts toward the place itself and the weekend around it.
- Higher per-guest experience; guests absorb travel & lodging.

## DECISION FOUR
## Planning Complexity

> [!insight] Decision Weight
> With low-stress as a stated priority, this decision carries more weight than usual. Venue type, more than location, decides how much we manage ourselves.

| VENUE TYPE | WHAT WE COORDINATE | PLANNER NEEDED? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| All-inclusive estate / resort | Catering, tables, staff, often lodging — bundled on site. | Optional |
| Hotel or country club | In-house catering & rentals; we source florals, photo, music. | Helpful |
| Blank-canvas barn / outdoor | Nearly everything — rentals, power, tents, every vendor. | Effectively required |

> [!tip] The Lowest-stress Path
> A venue that bundles the most, so we manage the fewest independent vendors.

## Timing & Sequence

2028 sounds far away. 'The decisions that protect it are not.

> [!insight] Strategic Booking
> Popular venues and the best vendors book 12–24 months out — sometimes more for peak Saturdays. Locking the venue early is what keeps every later choice open.

| Priority | Details |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Locks first** | Venue & date — everything else is downstream. |
| **Locks early** | Photographer, lodging block, planner if any. |
| **Time-sensitive** | Destination guests need 8–12 months' notice. |
| **Flexible** | Florals, menu, details — closer to the day. |

## THE SEQUENCE, ON A CALENDAR
### The Critical Path to 2028

- **NOW – MID 2026: Lock the vision**
    - Settle local vs. destination, size, and feel. Set a working budget range.
- **LATE 2026: Tour & book the venue**
    - Visit finalists, confirm a date.
    > [!important] Critical Decision
    > This is the decision everything waits on.
- **EARLY 2027: Secure key vendors**
    - Photographer, planner, lodging block — the ones that sell out first.
- **MID 2027: Save-the-dates out**
    - Especially for travel guests, who need runway to plan and budget.
- **2028: Details & the day**
    - Florals, menu, attire, timeline — the calm final stretch.

### THE COST OF WAITING
## What Disappears If We Wait

| IF WE DELAY... | WHAT QUIETLY CLOSES |
| :--- | :--- |
| Past late 2026 | Peak-season Saturdays at the most-wanted venues — including limited-inventory spots like Charlevoix. |
| Past early 2027 | The photographers and planners we'd actually choose: the rest is who's left. |
| Past mid 2027 | Hotel room blocks at sane rates; guests start paying more or staying farther out. |
| Too late, any stage | Calm. Compressed timelines are exactly the stress we said we wanted to avoid. |

> [!warning] Risk of Compressed Timelines
> Delaying past mid 2027 leads to compressed timelines and unnecessary stress—the exact outcome to be avoided.

### THE TRADEOFF UNDERNEATH
## Attendance vs. Intimacy

Cost and distance don't just shrink the guest list — they choose its character.

> [!important] The Venue Trade-off
> The further and pricier the venue, the fewer who come — but those who do invest a whole weekend. That can be exactly the intimate, elegant day we want, or a room that feels thinner than hoped.

| Location | Attendance Rate | Experience |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **NEAR-HOME** | ~85–90% of invited attend | A fuller room, lighter ask, more of everyone present for one evening. |
| **CHARLEVOIX OR THE ISLANDS** | ~45–65% attend | A smaller, committed group — and a multi-day experience. The farther the venue, the lower the count. |

## The Recommendation

### DECIDE THIS FIRST
> [!important] Priority One
> **Venue & date, by late 2026**
> It's the domino. Nothing else can be locked with confidence until it is.

### THE CALMEST PATH
> [!tip] Low Stress Option
> **An all-inclusive resort like Beaches**
> Free planner, one venue, elegance built in — best on low stress, if a smaller, committed guest list is acceptable.

### THE FULLER ROOM
> [!note] Maximizing Attendance
> **A full-service venue near home**
> Highest turnout and lowest guest cost — at the price of a bigger host budget and a bit more coordination.

### THEN SET THE NUMBER
> [!insight] Budget Alignment
> **Lock a budget to the venue type**
> Resorts shift cost to guests; near-home concentrates it on us. Pick the type, then the budget follows.
