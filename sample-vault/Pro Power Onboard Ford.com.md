How To Use Pro Power Onboard — Step by Step

1. Power On

When your vehicle is turned on in an open, well-ventilated area, you can send power where you need it. The easiest way to tell which system you have is from your center display (for 2.0 kW, 2.4 kW, 7.2 kW, and 9.6 kW systems). Select Features. Then choose Pro Power Onboard. Note that the Pro Power Onboard — 400 W system will not appear on your center display nor in the Ford app and is functional when you turn your vehicle on.

Follow the guidelines for your system. Refer to your Owner’s Manual for information, warnings, and precautions.*

400 W System

Turn on your vehicle.
The power outlet indicator illuminates on when the outlet is providing power.
If you plan to use your system for more than 30 minutes, switch off the 30 min Max Idle. You cannot permanently disable this feature and will need to switch it off each time you turn on your vehicle.

Select Vehicle from the settings menu from the center display.
Switch off the 30 min Max Idle.
F-150 2.0 kW, 2.4 kW, or 7.2 kW and Super Duty 2.0 kW systems

From your center display, choose Features. Select Pro Power Onboard and then select Generator Mode.
Or press the Pro Power Onboard hard button located on your dash (2.0 kW system) or above the center display (2.4 kW and 7.2 kW systems).
Cargo bed outlets will also need to be turned on by pressing the power button at the outlet to begin running power.
F-150 Lightning 2.4 kW and 9.6 kW systems

From your center display, choose Features. Select Pro Power Onboard and then select Generator Mode.
Turn on outlets at the Mega Power Frunk and cargo bed by pressing the power button at the outlet to begin running power.
E-Transit 2.4 kW system

From your center display, choose Features. Select Pro Power Onboard and then select Generator Mode.
Switch rear outlets on or off.
Alternately, instead of using the center display, you can press the power button at the rear outlet control to turn it on.
2. Monitor Your Power

You can easily keep track of usage and load on your center display and remotely through your Ford app. You’ll be notified if you trip a circuit breaker by pulling too much power. If you trip your circuit, unplug all devices and reset the breaker from the center display. For the 7.2 kW and 9.6 kW systems, you also have the option to reset the breakers from the cargo bed outlet by pressing the two reset buttons. #bookmark

Please note: The 400 W system does not offer center display or Ford app integration and controls.

Access View-Only Mode in the Ford app

With the return of Pro Power Onboard into the Ford app, you can access a view-only mode in the app. This will allow you to see your on/off status, power consumption, and any related warnings/errors. Additional features and functions are expected in later releases.

To access Pro Power Onboard in the Ford app:

Log in to the Ford app.
Tap the Vehicle screen.
Select the Pro Power Onboard tab.
3. Mobile Power

Charge up your toys, tools, and devices even while driving. Your Pro Power Onboard system is operational in motion. That means you can plug in on the way and arrive powered up using any of the Pro Power outlets during drive time. Note that F-150 Lightning frunk outlets have reduced output when the frunk is closed shut. Maximum power may be reduced when your vehicle is in Drive (D).

4. Idle Power

Help keep your equipment safe and charging while your pickup is parked in an open, well-ventilated area. You can remove your key, lock your truck, and idle while running Pro Power Onboard in Utility Idle mode for extended use, including essential home backup (with F-150® Hybrid PowerBoost™ truck with 7.2kW Pro Power Onboard™ and GenerLink transfer switch, sold separately).

How to turn on Utility Idle:

Select Features on your center display touchscreen, open Pro Power Onboard, select Generator Mode, and choose Utility Idle.
To remove your flip key:

Press the brake pedal once.
Turn the key to position 0.
Remove key and Pro Power Onboard will run until fuel or battery power is fully exhausted.
Please note: The 400 W system does not offer center display or Ford app integration and controls.

5. Pro Power Reserve on F-150 Lightning Truck

You can save battery range by setting a battery reserve limit so that you have enough charge to get home or to a public charging station to recharge your truck. This feature can also be used to maintain a minimum power level for essential home backup needs. Select your limit with the Pro Power Reserve control within the Pro Power Onboard feature on your center display or via the Ford app.
